New Jersey is home to many beautiful beaches. With 130 miles of coastline, there's no doubt that there would be a multitude of vacation spots for its inhabitants. I grew up in Toms River, NJ. My hometown is 13 minutes away from Seaside, one of New Jersey's most popular beach towns.

If Seaside sounds familiar, you might remember the MTV show "Jersey Shore". "Jersey Shore" showcased Italians spending their summer in Seaside, living directly in a shore house on the boardwalk. While the show may portray New Jerseyans in a negative live (having viewers who aren't from New Jersey think we're all "guidos") the show did portray the beautiful sights, beach, and attractions that Seaside has to offer.

While Seaside is certainly one of the most popular boardwalks in New Jersey, it's certainly not the only one. Other noteable and famous beaches and boardwalks in New Jersey are: Point Pleasant, Atlantic City, Ocean City, Wildwood, and Cape May.

Point Pleasant

Point Pleasant in one of New Jersey's most kid-friendly boardwalks with aquariums, mini golf, rides, games, candy shops, fun houses and more. Point Pleasant became a boardwalk in 1926 thanks to Charles Jenkinson. Nearly every attraction on the Point Pleasant Boardwalk is named after Jenkinson. Point Pleasant isn't only for families, with restauraunts like Martel's Tiki Bar, everyone is sure to have a fun time, whether you have kids or not.

Seaside

Definitely New Jersey's livliest boardwalks, Seaside is separated into Seaside Heights and Seaside Park. The Seaside Heights bordwalk is lined with bars, tattoo parlors, food stands and rides. Seaside Park has shops, games, and is considered the tamer of the two, even if the "Jersey Shore" cast did tear Seaside up for a few summers.

Atlantic City

New Jersey's (and America's) first boardwalk, Atlantic City is the queen of all boardwalks. There are many attractions in Atlantic City, from endless casinos, rides, food stands, and even music halls, you'll never be short of anything fun to do in AC. Atlantic City was even home to the first Miss America pageant in 1921

Ocean City

Dubbed "America's Greatest Family Resort", Ocean City is a G-rated boardwalk for the whole family to enjoy. While the boardwalk has food stands, rides, and mini golf, Ocean City doesn't have any "Jersey Shore" t-shirt stands. As an added bonus, Ocean City has a boardwalk movie theatre called The Moorlyn.

Wildwood

The "Wildwoods" sign and gigantic beach balls is a staple of the Wildwood boardwalk. Two miles of food, fun, rides, shops, and piers. The Jersey shore has been the hub of summer fun for an endless amount of decades. However, sometimes living close to the coastline doesn't always have its perks.

Hurricane Sandy

On October 29, 2012, Hurricane Sandy hit the shores of New Jersey. Everything New Jerseyans knew and loved about the Jersey Shore seemed to change. Hurricane Sandy took the shore by storm, tearing many of its beloved beaches and boardwalks to shreds, like Seaside, pictured below in the before and after pictures.
